Mao Bayotzin

Philippines | Second engineer

Mao is a Second Engineer Officer but also an avid reader, gamer, and explorer from the Philippines. Although Mao initially aspired to study chemical engineering in college, he enrolled in maritime courses for financial stability and learned to love his job. He shared his advice for incoming seafarers with us:

1. Ask questions. The most important thing you will display onboard is your humbleness and willingness to learn from your peers.

2. Be cooperative. Get along with your crew every day in order to have an enjoyable trip.

3. Have courage. Seafaring is not a walk in the park. Seamen must be inspired by the profession. The paycheck will not be enough to take you to new horizons.

Mao continues to educate new seafarers through an organization he founded called The Millennial Seafarers (@themillennialseaferers). He encourages his students to maximize their learnings on each trip and to continue using technical instructions they learned in school.

Mao looks forward to new destinations and adventures (as well as good internet and Kawali). His favorite destination so far is the port of Rio de Janeiro. He says seeing the Christ the Redeemer statue was breathtaking and the beaches brought much-needed rest to the seafarers on the ship. Mao hopes to visit Venice sometime in the near future.

From everyone at Seafair, we wish you the best of luck, Mao! We can’t wait to see what you do next.
